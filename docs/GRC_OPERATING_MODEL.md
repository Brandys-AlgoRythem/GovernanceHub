# Governance Hub GRC Operating Model

Governance Hub is a local-first business GRC workspace built with sample data so a reviewer can enter dummy business information, open the workspace, and see how the system would organize governance work.

It is designed as a portfolio implementation inspired by a full governance application, not as a flat demo page.

## Core Operating Areas

A comprehensive small-business GRC workspace needs coverage across governance, risk, compliance, records, evidence, vendors, expenses, continuity, and audit readiness.

Governance Hub organizes those needs into connected modules.

## 1. Business Foundation

The workspace needs a place to capture the basic identity of the business.

Coverage includes:

- Entity name
- Entity type
- Formation state
- Governance lead
- Registered agent concept
- Ownership and role map
- Operating cadence
- Formation record status

Current location:

- Landing workspace setup
- Business Profile
- Dashboard workspace snapshot

## 2. Obligations Management

A business needs to know what must be filed, renewed, reviewed, or confirmed.

Coverage includes:

- Obligation title
- Category
- Jurisdiction
- Frequency
- Due date
- Owner
- Priority
- Status
- Evidence requirement

Current location:

- Obligations Tracker
- Dashboard deadline table

## 3. Governance Vault

A business needs one place to understand what records exist and what is missing.

Coverage includes:

- Formation records
- Operating agreements
- tax records
- policies
- licenses
- insurance records
- contracts
- storage location
- review date

Current location:

- Governance Vault
- Documents data model

## 4. Licenses and Permits

A business needs renewal visibility before licenses expire or proof goes missing.

Coverage includes:

- License name
- Jurisdiction
- License number
- Owner
- Expiration date
- Renewal status
- Linked proof
- Notes

Current location:

- Licenses and Permits
- Licenses data model

## 5. Risk Register

A business needs a practical risk register that connects risks to owners and mitigation.

Coverage includes:

- Risk title
- Category
- Likelihood
- Impact
- Inherent risk
- Mitigation
- Owner
- Residual risk
- Status

Current location:

- Risk Register
- Dashboard open risk metrics

A business needs repeatable controls so governance does not depend on memory.

Coverage includes:

- Control name

- Owner
- Frequency
- Evidence required

- Status

Current location:

- Controls / SOP Library
- Controls data model

GRC work needs proof. The evidence tracker shows what supports obligations, controls, expenses, documents, risks, and licenses.

Coverage includes:

- Evidence title
- Supported item
- Evidence type
- Owner
- Collection date
- Expiration date
- Status
- Linked document

Current location:

- Evidence Tracker
- Dashboard evidence metrics

## 8. Expense Support

Business expenses need receipts, categories, review status, and support records.

Coverage includes:

- Expense date
- Vendor
- Category
- Amount
- Frequency
- Receipt status
- Linked evidence
- Quarter
- Notes

Current location:

- Expenses data model

## 9. Vendor and Dependency Awareness

A business needs to know which outside parties affect operations.

Coverage includes:

- Vendor name
- Category
- Owner

## 10. Continuity and Audit Readiness

A business needs a way to see whether records, owners, proof, renewals, and risks are ready for review.

Coverage includes:

- Governance health score
- Upcoming deadlines
- Missing proof
- Open risks
- Pending renewals
- Backup ownership
- Recent activity

Current location:

- Executive Dashboard
- Roadmap
- Recent Activity feed

## Workspace Behavior

The landing page allows a reviewer to enter dummy business information and save it locally in the browser.

That sample workspace data can be reflected inside the app so the experience feels like a working business GRC workspace rather than a static showcase.

## Portfolio Positioning

Governance Hub should be presented as a working local-first portfolio app with sample data.

It should not claim to be a production compliance platform. It should show how a comprehensive business GRC system could be structured, navigated, validated, and expanded.
